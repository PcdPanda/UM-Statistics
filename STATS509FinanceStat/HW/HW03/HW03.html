(a)

\[ E[\hat f_b(x)]=E[\frac{1}{100}\sum_{i=1}^{100}K_b(x-x_i)]=E[\int_{-\infty}^\infty K_b(x-x_i)f(x_i)\mathrm d x_i] \]

Based on our setting of \(K_b\) and \(f(x)\) \[ E[\hat f_b(x)]=\frac{1}{w}\int_{x-w/2}^{x+w/2}f(x_i)\mathrm dx_i=\frac{1}{w}(F_{ged,1.5}(x+w/2)-F_{ged,1.5}(x-w/2)) \]

(b)

The bias should be \(E[\hat f_b(x)]-f(x)=\frac{1}{w}[F_{ged,1.5}(x+w/2)-F_{ged,1.5}(x-w/2)]-f_{ged,1.5}(x)\)

(c)

\[ Var[\hat f_b(x)]=\frac{1}{100}Var[K_b(x-x_i)]=\frac{1}{100}[E[(K_b(x-x_i))^2]-(E[K_b(x-x_i)])^2] \]

For \(E[K_b(x-x_i)]^2\), from (a) we have \(\frac{1}{w^2}[F_{ged,1.5}(x+w/2)-F_{ged,1.5}(x-w/2)]^2\)

For \(E[(K_b(x-x_i))^2]\), we have \[ \int_{-\infty}^\infty [K_b(x-x_i)]^2f(x_i)\mathrm d x_i=\frac{1}{w^2}(F_{ged,1.5}(x+w/2)-F_{ged,1.5}(x-w/2)) \] Then for the standard deviation, we have \[ \sigma=\frac{1}{10w}\sqrt{[F_{ged,1.5}(x+w/2)-F_{ged,1.5}(x-w/2)]-[F_{ged,1.5}(x+w/2)-F_{ged,1.5}(x-w/2)]^2} \]
